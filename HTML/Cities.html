  • Chennai, Tamil Nadu


    How to reach?

    By Air: Chennai international airport is 7 kilometres away from the Chennai city. This is the 3rd busiest airport in India. Frequent domestic as well as international flights arrive here. Anna terminal receives international flights from various major cities in the world. Kamaraj terminal, which is at a distance of 150 metres from the Anna terminal, has domestic flights connecting Chennai with major Indian cities.
    By Rail: Chennai Central and Chennai Egmore are the main railway stations in the city receiving regular trains from various major cities of India such as Delhi, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, and Kolkata etc.
    By Road: City is well connected with other cities in India with a decent road network. Various national highways from Chennai connect with Bengaluru (330km), Trichy (326km), Puducherry (162km) and Tiruvallur (47km). One can use car rental services or state transport buses to solve the purpose of how to reach Chennai. There are various buses operating from other cities to Chennai like Bangalore to Chennai Bus, Calicut to Chennai bus service etc


    What to do?

    Shop clothes as they are very good and enjoy the local cuisine which are the South Indian Dishes which are very famous throughout India as well great beach food and visit temples for spirituality


    What to see?

    • Kapaleeshwar Temple - A must visit for devotees and tourists.
    • Kalikambal Temple - A religious temple for devotees and tourists.
    • Sri Parthasarathy Temple - A religious temple with great art work on it.
    • Viswaroopa Adhivyadhihara Sri Bhaktha Anjaneyaswami Temple

  • Gangtok, Sikkim


    How to reach?

    By Air: Nearest airport is Bagdogra,West Bengal abut 124Km away.It has flights from all major cities,there are helicoptors facility available between Bagdogra and Gangtok.
    By Rail: New Jalpaiguri(NJP) is the closet railway station from Gangtok which is 117Km away.The railway station is well linked with both major and minor parts of the country.
    By Road: Gangtok is well connected to major cities like Siliguri,Darjeeling through national highway 31A.Regular Sikkim state transport bus service are available.


    What to do?

    There are lots of thing to do here as you can do treks for high-altitude, Ropeways are also available at some places and you can have Helicopters for eagle-eye mountains


    What to see?

    • Tsomgo Lake, Himalayan Zoological Park, Enchey Monastery, Tashi View Point, Fambong La Wildlife Sanctuary, Tsomgo Lake is located in the north eastern part of Sikkim about 35km from city.
    • This lake has been worshipped as a holy lake by Sikkimese

  • Shimla, Himachal Pradesh


    How to reach?

    By Air: The airport is at Jubbarhatti, 23 km from Shimla city and is connected to Delhi. Taxi/cabs are available.
    By Rail: Nearest station is Kalka which connects the city with different parts of the country by rail line. There are quite a good number of trains from the cities like Delhi and Calcutta. From Kalka station, one can take the pleasure of toy train as well as can hire private cabs to explore the beauty of this city.
    By Road: It is well connected with the cities like Chandigarh, Delhi, Dehradun and Kalka though roadways. You can hire taxis as well as state buses to reach the hill station from these cities. There are even regular bus services from the nearby places to Shimla such as Kullu (235 km), Manali (280 km), Delhi (370 km) and Ambala (154 km). From the capital of India, one can even take luxury buses and cabs which will provide a comfortable journey.


    What to do?

    You can do shopping of Shawls and tweeds. The best thing is that we can river Rafting,Treking and Ice Skating in Shimla.

  • Panaji, Goa


    How to reach?

    By Air: Goa International Airport, Dabolim
    By Rail: Nearest railway station: Karmali Railway Station, Madgaon Junction Railway Station and Vasco da Gama. These railway stations are well linked with Mumbai and then other major parts of the country as well.
    By Road: Kadamba bus stand of Panaji is considered as the main bus station. Goa has its own bus service that connects major cities in and out of the state.


    What to do?

    • Visit the beaches
    • Try Water sport
    • Visit the architectural buildings
    • Taste the spicy local Goan cuisine.
